# Universal Robots UR10

## Overview
The UR10 is a large collaborative robot designed for applications requiring extended reach and higher payload capacity. It's ideal for palletizing, packaging, and machine tending applications.

## Specifications

### Physical Dimensions
- **Reach**: 1300 mm
- **Weight**: 33.5 kg
- **Footprint**: Ø 190 mm
- **Mounting**: Floor, wall, ceiling, or angle

### Payload Capacity
- **Maximum Payload**: 12.5 kg
- **Payload at Maximum Speed**: 10 kg
- **Optimal Payload**: 5-10 kg for best performance
- **Payload at Maximum Reach**: 12.5 kg

### Degrees of Freedom
- **DOF**: 6 (revolute joints)

### Performance
- **Repeatability**: ±0.05 mm
- **Maximum Joint Speed**: 120°/s (joints 1-3), 180°/s (joints 4-6)
- **Maximum TCP Speed**: 1 m/s
- **Maximum TCP Acceleration**: 15 m/s²
- **Typical Cycle Time**: ~4 seconds for standard pick-and-place

### Joint Ranges
- **Joint 1 (Base)**: ±360° (infinite rotation)
- **Joint 2 (Shoulder)**: ±360° (infinite rotation)
- **Joint 3 (Elbow)**: ±360° (infinite rotation)
- **Joint 4 (Wrist 1)**: ±360° (infinite rotation)
- **Joint 5 (Wrist 2)**: ±360° (infinite rotation)
- **Joint 6 (Wrist 3)**: ±360° (infinite rotation)

### Tool Interface
- **Payload Capacity**: 12.5 kg (includes tool weight)
- **I/O Ports**: 2 digital inputs, 2 digital outputs, 2 analog inputs
- **Power Supply**: 12V DC (600 mA), 24V DC (600 mA)
- **Communication**: Ethernet, Modbus TCP, PROFINET, EtherNet/IP

## Use Cases

### Primary Applications
- **Palletizing**: Box stacking, pallet building (up to 12 kg)
- **Machine Tending**: Large CNC machine loading/unloading
- **Packaging**: Heavy product handling, case packing
- **Material Handling**: Bin picking, part transfer of heavier items
- **Welding**: Arc welding, spot welding with standard torches
- **Assembly**: Large component assembly
- **Pick and Place**: Heavy-duty sorting and handling
- **Dispensing**: Glue, sealant application for large parts

### Ideal For
- Parts weighing 5-12 kg
- Long reach applications (up to 1.3 m)
- Palletizing and depalletizing
- Heavy-duty machine tending
- Large workspace coverage
- Applications requiring extended vertical reach
- Medium to high-volume production
- Collaborative applications with heavier tools

### Not Recommended For
- Very heavy payloads (>12.5 kg)
- Ultra-compact workspaces (large footprint when extended)
- Ultra-high-precision applications (<0.05 mm)
- Ultra-high-speed applications (cycle time <1 sec)
- Space-constrained benchtop applications

## Safety Features
- **Safety Rating**: Category 3, PLd per EN ISO 13849-1
- 17 configurable safety functions
- Built-in force and torque sensing in all joints
- Advanced collision detection
- Configurable safety zones (tool position, speed, boundaries)
- Emergency stop and protective stop
- Power and force limiting
- Safety I/O for integration with safety sensors
- Reduced speed and force in collaborative mode

## Communication & Control
- **Control Interface**: URScript, RTDE (Real-Time Data Exchange)
- **Programming**: 
  - PolyScope touchscreen (12" tablet)
  - Python via URX or RTDE
  - C++ via UR Client Library
  - ROS/ROS2 drivers
  - Direct socket communication
- **Real-time Control**: 500 Hz servo rate, 125 Hz trajectory control
- **Supported Frameworks**: ROS, ROS2, Genesis, MoveIt
- **Network**: 100 Mbps Ethernet, Modbus TCP, PROFINET, EtherNet/IP

## Genesis Integration
- **MJCF/URDF**: Compatible with standard UR10 models
- **End-Effector Link**: `wrist_3_link` or `tool0`
- **Home Position**: `[0, -1.57, 0, -1.57, 0, 0]` (typical)
- **Tool Flange**: ISO 9409-1-50-4-M6

- **Service**: Modular design for easy joint replacement

## Advantages
- Extended reach (1.3 m) for large work areas
- High payload capacity (12.5 kg) for heavier tasks
- Safe collaborative operation despite size
- Excellent for palletizing applications
- Flexible mounting options
- Easy programming
- Large ecosystem of compatible tools and accessories

## Considerations
- Larger footprint and weight than UR3/UR5
- Slightly lower repeatability (±0.05 mm vs ±0.03 mm)
- Higher power consumption
- Requires more space when fully extended
- May need safety risk assessment for faster collaborative speeds
